个人简介

About

从药学、化学到医学转化研究

From pharmacy and chemistry to medical translational research

本科阶段接受药学专业训练，并在化学竞赛中建立了较系统的化学思维。 硕士阶段围绕环肽全合成、结构计算设计和抗炎活性评价开展研究， 逐步形成从分子设计、化学合成到生命功能评价的研究基础。

2021年进入南京大学化学化工学院攻读有机化学博士期间， 我将研究进一步拓展到蛋白-蛋白相互作用、界面识别和功能调控， 关注分子机制如何被转化为可设计的工程工具。

2025年7月入职宁波大学附属第一医院中心实验室后，我任特聘副研究员、 独立课题组长。依托医院临床资源，我希望以金属相关机制与工程平台为主线， 面向跟腱损伤、病理性生物矿化、口腔疾病、肿瘤靶向和PET/CT分子影像等问题开展研究。

My undergraduate training in pharmacy and chemistry competitions built a systematic chemical foundation. During my master's training, I worked on cyclic peptide total synthesis, computational structural design, and anti-inflammatory activity evaluation, forming a foundation that connects molecular design, chemical synthesis, and biological function.

In 2021, I joined the School of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering at Nanjing University for doctoral training. My work gradually moved toward mechanisms by which protein-protein interactions participate in biological processes, and how these mechanisms can be engineered.

Since joining the Central Laboratory of The First Affiliated Hospital of Ningbo University in July 2025, I have been working as a Distinguished Associate Research Fellow and independent PI. My current work uses metal-centered mechanisms and engineering platforms to address tendon injury, pathological biomineralization, oral diseases, cancer targeting, and PET/CT molecular imaging.

研究方向

Research

金属如何连接疾病机制、生物医学工程与临床诊疗？

How do metals connect disease mechanisms, biomedical engineering, and clinical theranostics?

研究从临床问题出发，再回到金属离子、金属配位、金属界面和放射金属等科学命题。 我希望把疾病机制、生物医学工程和分子影像放在同一条研究线上， 让机制发现能够转化为局部递送、组织修复和分子诊疗工具。 目前相关合作主要来自骨科中心、口腔科和核医学科。

I use metal ions, metal coordination, and radiometals as entry points to connect disease mechanisms, biomedical engineering, and molecular imaging. Mechanisms, engineering platforms, and theranostic tools are organized around metals. Clinical questions are translated into problems involving metal ions, metal coordination, metal interfaces, or radiometals. Current clinical inputs come mainly from orthopedics, stomatology, and nuclear medicine.

01

金属作为机制变量

Metals as mechanistic variables

围绕病理性结晶、金属离子稳态和组织微环境，研究金属相关过程如何影响炎症、矿化和组织修复。

This direction asks how pathological crystals, metal-ion homeostasis, and tissue microenvironments shape inflammation, mineralization, and tissue repair.

  • 痛风晶体、牙结石等病理性矿化过程
  • 金属组学与金属离子细胞生物学
  • 合作科室：骨科中心、中心试验室
  • Pathological mineralization, including gout crystals and dental calculus
  • Metallomics and metal-ion cell biology
  • Clinical inputs: orthopedics and stomatology
02

金属作为工程节点

Metals as engineering nodes

围绕跟腱损伤和组织修复，发展以金属配位、金属多酚网络和MOF/纳米酶为核心的局部递送平台。

This direction develops injectable and local delivery platforms based on metal coordination, metal-phenolic networks, MOFs, and nanozymes for tendon injury and tissue repair.

  • 压电水凝胶、3D打印支架、静电纺丝体系
  • 微针、微球等递送缓释系统
  • 合作科室：骨科中心
  • Piezoelectric hydrogels, 3D-printed scaffolds, and electrospun fibers
  • Microneedle- and microsphere-based sustained delivery systems
  • Clinical input: orthopedics
03

金属作为诊疗工具

Metals as theranostic tools

围绕肿瘤靶向和PET/CT分子影像，发展放射金属配位平台、多肽和binder设计工具。

This direction develops radiometal, metal-coordination, and binder-design tools for cancer targeting and PET/CT molecular imaging.

  • PPI界面肽段、从头设计binder与噬菌体展示
  • 基于MOF的PET/CT成像示踪剂
  • 合作科室：核医学科、口腔科
  • PPI-interface peptides, de novo binder design, and phage display
  • MOF-based PET/CT imaging probes
  • Clinical inputs: nuclear medicine and stomatology
